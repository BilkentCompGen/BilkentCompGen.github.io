### After Alkan Lab

After graduating from Alkan Lab, Can Firtina started as a PhD student at [ETH Zurich](https://ethz.ch/en.html) in 2018.

Can Firtina is currently an Assistant Professor in the [Department of Computer Science](https://www.cs.umd.edu) at the [University of Maryland, College Park](https://www.umd.edu) (UMD). He leads the [STORM Research Group](https://storm.cs.umd.edu) at UMD. His research focuses on algorithms and computing systems for bioinformatics.

His interests span bioinformatics, artificial intelligence, and computer architecture. He works on a broad range of problems to address key challenges in computer science and computational biology. He designs methods and builds systems that use emerging computing paradigms and memory technologies to enable fast, accurate, energy-efficient, and real-time analysis of biological data. He is also interested in developing solutions for applications such as genome editing. He received the ETH Doctoral Medal Prize in 2025, the highest prize awarded for doctoral dissertations at ETH Zurich. His research has been published in major bioinformatics and computer architecture venues.

Previously, he was a Senior Researcher and Lecturer at [ETH Zurich](https://ethz.ch/en.html), where he taught courses on [accelerating genome analysis with hardware–algorithm co-design](https://safari.ethz.ch/projects_and_seminars/spring2025/doku.php?id=bioinformatics). His lecture videos and materials are available on YouTube. He received his PhD from ETH Zurich under the supervision of [Prof. Onur Mutlu](https://people.inf.ethz.ch/omutlu/) in the [SAFARI Research Group](https://safari.ethz.ch).
